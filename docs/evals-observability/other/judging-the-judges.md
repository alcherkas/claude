# Judging the Judges: evaluating alignment and vulnerabilities in LLMs-as-judges

> **Excerpt from the original:** Across LLM judges, position bias is "not merely random variation" — it is a systematic vulnerability, tracing back to the MT-Bench / Zheng et al. line of work. The study examines how well judge models align with human preferences and where they break down.

**Type:** Peer-reviewed academic (arXiv 2406.07791).

**Pattern / technique:** Corroborates the [CALM](calm-quantifying-biases-in-llm-as-a-judge.md) and [survey](a-survey-on-llm-as-a-judge.md) findings on judge bias from an alignment/vulnerability angle — reinforcing that LLM-as-judge verdicts need human calibration and order-swapping before they gate releases or feed training.
