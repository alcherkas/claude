# A Survey on LLM-as-a-Judge

> **Excerpt from the original:** Formalizes LLM-as-a-judge as a defined paradigm — `E ← P_LLM(x ⊕ C)` — where `x` is the input, `C` the context/prompt-template/criteria, and `⊕` the combination operator. It documents that "LLM evaluators exhibit position bias in pairwise evaluations," canonically mitigated by "swapping the contents and averaging the scores."

**Type:** Academic survey (arXiv 2411.15594).

**Pattern / technique:** Establishes LLM-as-a-judge as a *formal* evaluation method rather than an ad-hoc practice, and catalogues its bias taxonomy and mitigation strategies. Read alongside [CALM](calm-quantifying-biases-in-llm-as-a-judge.md) (quantified biases) and [Judging the Judges](judging-the-judges.md) (vulnerability analysis).
