# The Agentic Operating Model: governing autonomous AI at scale

> **Excerpt from the original:** This Berkeley *California Management Review* article (Mar 2026) proposes the **Agentic Operating Model (AOM)** — a four-layer governance framework: **Cognitive, Coordination, Control, and Governance** layers — for operating autonomous agents at enterprise scale. Its load-bearing shift is from **Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) to Human-on-the-Loop (HOTL)** supervision: humans define objectives, constraints, and escalation thresholds while agents operate independently, with **confidence thresholds, behavioral baselines, and guardrail agents** that selectively block high-risk actions in real time rather than approving every action. Its Governance layer requires each agent to have a **clear business owner, a defined risk profile, and documented decision boundaries**.

**Relevance to agentic orchestration & workflows:** The governance/operating-model counterpart to the agent factory — *how you run* a fleet responsibly once you can *produce* one. The **HITL→HOTL** shift directly extends this topic's [on-the-loop](../../context-engineering/martin-fowler/humans-and-agents-in-software-engineering-loops.md) thread, and the four-layer model frames the same concern that Gartner quantifies as **agent sprawl** (it projects >150,000 agents per Fortune 500 enterprise by 2028, up from <15 in 2025, with ungoverned sprawl risking misinformation, oversharing, and data loss). The "humans steer, agents execute" reframing is independently echoed by McKinsey and Deloitte. *Evidence: high-confidence for the framework's content — the four layers, HITL→HOTL, and per-agent ownership/risk/boundaries all verified unanimous 3-0 (June 2026). Caveat: a proposed operating model (a consensus thesis whose real-world adoption is still early), and the source is a management journal, not an implementation spec.*
