# Why Do Multi-Agent LLM Systems Fail?

> **Excerpt from the original:** A first empirically grounded study of failures in multi-agent LLM systems (MAS). Analyzing popular open-source frameworks across many tasks, the authors derive **MAST** (the Multi-Agent System Failure Taxonomy): 14 distinct failure modes organized into three categories — (1) specification and system-design issues, (2) inter-agent misalignment, and (3) task verification and termination — and find failures are driven as much by design and coordination as by individual model capability.

**Relevance to agentic orchestration & workflows:** The load-bearing academic source for the "when does multi-agent break" question — it grounds the failure-mode and maturity claims that vendor design-guidance can't, and gives a vocabulary for diagnosing why an orchestration fell over. Pair with Cognition's "Don't Build Multi-Agents" and Anthropic's coding caveat. *Maturity: non-peer-reviewed arXiv preprint (Cemri et al., UC Berkeley, 2025) — treat as early research (backfill).*
